- City/county meeting attachments can contain restrictive covenants that are purely public-property use restrictions rather than HOA instruments. The latest Maury reject was a Howard Field recreation/library-use restriction for public land.
- Voluntary neighborhood associations should stay out unless the document creates a mandatory residential common-interest association. Historic College Hill Neighborhood Association bylaws in Maryville were rejected on that basis.
- Ball Homes and similar builder-hosted scans can be bankable when the rendered first page shows Tennessee county/state recorder evidence. Clover Meadow was a Blount County keeper even though text extraction initially produced no state evidence.
- Source snippets can route county correctly even when the deterministic cleaner accepts an out-of-state shared-name PDF. The Sullivan pass accepted a Pasco County, Florida master-deed packet from a school/campus storage host; reject these unless the PDF itself has Tennessee evidence.
- When a high-signal PDF is already present under TN `_unknown-county`, avoid creating a second county-routed manifest unless doing a deliberate migration. Recent examples: St. Ives and Chestnut Cove.
- `static.crye-leike.com` can expose bankable scanned Cumberland/Putnam-area covenant amendments, but Python `requests` currently fails TLS handshake where curl succeeds. The Deer Creek amendment was verified visually but not banked from that host; look for a mirror before retrying through the probe writer.
- Anderson/Robertson gap searches trigger many shared-name out-of-state HOA documents. Recent rejects included Oak Ridge in Oklahoma, Park Meadow in Alabama, Greenbrier in Texas, Saddletree in Alabama, Cedar Hills in Oregon, and The Lakes in Polk County, Florida.
- Anderson/Robertson accepted-looking municipal/government false positives included Oak Ridge/NRC zoning materials, Ridgetop/Sumner/Robertson subdivision regulations, and Tennessee legal/government HOA reports. Keep rejecting these even when the cleaner extracts HOA terminology from model ordinances or subdivision-regulation templates.
- Sevier/Jefferson gap searches are rich in scanned county-recorded covenants, but the cleaner accepted several non-bankable rows and rejected many real scans. Recent rejects to keep out: generic `Tips on Writing Bylaws`, Sevier ridge/hillside regulations, county/city subdivision and zoning rules, Sycamore Ridge in Kentucky, and Preserve at Oakland / Jefferson County records without Tennessee evidence.
- Possessive association names can create bad bank slugs such as `wild-pear-shores-s`. Normalize `Homeowner's Association` to `Homeowners Association` in curated lead files before probing; if a bad prefix lands, rebank under the clean name and remove the bad prefix immediately.
- Cocke/Grainger gap searches are also out-of-state noisy. Recent rejects included Club Rio in Florida, Preserve at Newport in Harris County Texas, Newport/Newport POA materials tied to Chatham County Georgia, Rutledge Landing in Wake County North Carolina, Thornhill hits outside Tennessee, and generic governing-document primers.
- Pindell-Wilson/Rutledge URLs can look promising for Grainger because of the county name, but the tested `Rutledge Landing` documents are North Carolina. Do not bank unless a future PDF itself shows Tennessee/Grainger evidence.
