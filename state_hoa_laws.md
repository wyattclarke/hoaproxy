# State HOA / Condo / Coop Laws Corpus

**Research questions:**
1. **Document access & transparency** — what documents any homeowner is entitled to see, procedures, fees, and restrictions on sharing.
2. **Proxy voting** — whether allowed, directed vs. undirected, submission requirements, duration, limits on holders, recording requirements.

**Scope:** HOAs, condominium associations, and cooperative boards. Law may live in HOA-specific statutes, Condominium Acts, Cooperative Corporation Acts, and/or general Nonprofit Corporation Acts.

**Last updated:** 2026-03-01
**Status:** All 51 jurisdictions complete. See comparison table below.

---

## Cross-State Comparison: Key Patterns

### Document Access

| Category | States / Notes |
|---|---|
| **Strong statutory access rights** (10-day response window, no stated-purpose requirement) | AZ (10 days), CA (10 days current / 30 days older), CO (no purpose required), FL (10 days HOA / 10 days condo), IL (10 days condo), NC (reasonable fee + photocopy/electronic), NV (21 days max, $25/hr cap), WA (10 days, max 21 days) |
| **UCIOA/UCA framework states** (generally reasonable access, moderate protections) | AK, CO, CT, DE, MN, VT, WA, WV — all follow 1982 or 2008 UCIOA which mandates reasonable access |
| **Governing documents are public records** (recorded with county) | Most states — recorded governing documents are generally accessible via county recorder |
| **Governing documents NOT public records** | Nebraska (notable exception — documents kept with HOA, not recorded) |
| **No meaningful HOA-specific access statute** (relies on bylaws + general nonprofit law) | AR, IA, KY, LA, MA, MS, MT, ND, SD, WY and others with no comprehensive HOA act |
| **Audit requirements** | Michigan (associations with >$20k annual revenue must have CPA audit/review); NC (financial statements for prior 3 years) |
| **Records withheld in all states** | Attorney-client privilege, personnel matters, pending litigation, individual owner files of other owners |
| **Key unique features** | CA: penalties up to $500/request for non-compliance; NV: Ombudsman enforcement; WI: 100+ unit associations must maintain member-only website; UT: HOA Registry (state database) |

### Proxy Voting

| Category | States / Notes |
|---|---|
| **Proxies banned after declarant control ends** | **AZ only** — extremely strict; after developer period, no proxies for condos OR planned communities; absentee ballots substitute |
| **Proxies banned for board elections** | AZ (all votes post-declarant), FL condominiums (residential only; general proxies ok for HOAs), IL (CICAA board elections), MD condos (undirected proxies cannot vote for directors; directed required), NV (board elections prohibited) |
| **Directed proxies required for board elections** | MD (undirected proxies limited to quorum and general business; only directed or in-person votes count for director elections) |
| **Both directed and undirected explicitly recognized** | CA, CT, VT, WA (WUCIOA) — most progressive treatment |
| **Proxy holder must be a member** | CA (Davis-Stirling) — proxyholder must be an association member |
| **Strict limits on who can hold a proxy** | NV — proxy only to immediate family, tenant residing in community, another unit owner in community, or authorized delegate |
| **Proxy cap (max % one person can hold)** | NH — 10% of votes cast in 20+ unit condos; ID — 50% max (2025 legislation) |
| **Proxy requires witness** | DC — unusual requirement: proxy signatures must be witnessed by a person who signs their full name and address |
| **Proxy duration: 11 months** | AL (nonprofit law), CO (CCIOA), ID, IL, MS, NE, WA |
| **Proxy duration: 1 year** | AK, DE, RI |
| **Proxy duration: 90 days (specific meeting + 90 days)** | FL (HOA), IA |
| **Proxy duration: 180 days** | MD, WI |
| **Proxy duration: for specific meeting only** | NJ (unless adjourned), IA, FL HOA |
| **Proxy duration: rolling annual (year-to-year)** | OK (unusual — proxies roll forward annually until revoked) |
| **Proxy terminates at adjournment** | NH |
| **Proxy max 3 years** | ID |
| **Revocation: actual notice required** | Most states — the standard is "actual notice of revocation to the presiding officer"; general written revocation without notice to presiding officer is typically insufficient |
| **No proxy statute (governed by bylaws)** | Many states with no comprehensive HOA act: AR, KY, LA, MT, ND, OR, SC, SD, WY |

### Cooperative (Co-op) Boards

Most states have **no specific coop statute**. Coops are typically governed by:
- General nonprofit or business corporation law
- The coop's own proprietary lease, bylaws, and house rules

**Notable exceptions:**
- **New York:** Two separate legal regimes — BCL (Business Corporation Law) co-ops and CCL (Cooperative Corporations Law) co-ops have meaningfully different rules. BCL § 609 authorizes proxies in BCL co-ops; BCL § 609 does **not** apply to CCL co-ops.
- **Maryland:** Has a Cooperative Housing Corporation Act (Md. Code, Corps. & Assocs. §§ 5-6B-01 et seq.)
- **Virginia:** Has the Real Estate Cooperative Act (Va. Code §§ 55.1-2100 et seq.) — one of the more comprehensive coop-specific statutes
- **States covering coops under UCIOA:** AK, CO, CT, DE, MN, VT, WA, WV — UCIOA explicitly covers cooperatives as a form of common interest community

## Alabama

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA statute: Alabama Homeowners' Association Act, Ala. Code §§ 35-20-1 through 35-20-14 (applies to HOAs created on or after Jan 1, 2016)
- Condo statute: Alabama Uniform Condominium Act, Ala. Code §§ 35-8A-101 et seq. (applies to condos created after Jan 1, 1991)
- Coop statute: No specific coop statute found; general nonprofit corporation law applies
- General nonprofit/org statute: Alabama Nonprofit Corporation Law, Ala. Code § 10A-3-2.05 et seq.
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: Adopted Uniform Condominium Act (UCA) as basis for the 1991 Condo Act; no UCIOA adoption

### Document Access & Transparency
**HOAs (post-2016):** The Alabama Homeowners' Association Act requires HOAs formed on or after January 1, 2016 to file governing documents (CC&Rs, bylaws, amendments) with the Secretary of State through the local Probate Judge's office, making them public records. All HOA governing documents are also recorded with the county Probate Judge's office and thus available as public land records.

**Condominiums:** Ala. Code § 35-8A-318 governs association records. Unit owners have the right to inspect association records, including meeting minutes of board meetings (though owners do not have the right to attend all board meetings). Specific procedures, fees, and timelines are not well-specified in secondary sources; the full text of § 35-8A-318 governs.

**Older/pre-2016 HOAs:** Alabama has no comprehensive statute governing pre-2016 HOAs. Document access rights depend entirely on the association's governing documents.

**Restrictions on sharing:** No specific state law restrictions on redistribution of HOA documents found; general copyright principles would apply.

### Proxy Voting
**HOAs (nonprofit corporation context):** Under Ala. Code § 10A-3-2.05(b) (Alabama Nonprofit Corporation Law), a member may vote by proxy. Requirements:
- Proxy must be executed in writing by the member or their authorized attorney-in-fact
- Valid for 11 months from execution unless the proxy specifies a different period
- Member may revoke proxy by attending the meeting in person, voting in person, or providing actual notice of revocation to the presiding officer

**Condominiums (§ 35-8A-310):** Proxy voting is addressed in the Uniform Condominium Act. Budgets require approval by a majority of unit owners present in person or by proxy. Directed vs. undirected distinction and specific procedural requirements are governed by the statute and bylaws.

**Note:** Alabama has no post-declarant-control proxy ban (unlike Arizona). Proxies appear generally permitted by statute.

- [Alabama HOA Act, Ala. Code § 35-20-1](https://law.justia.com/codes/alabama/title-35/chapter-20/)
- [Alabama Uniform Condominium Act, Ala. Code § 35-8A-310](https://law.justia.com/codes/alabama/title-35/chapter-8a/)
- [Alabama Secretary of State – HOA FAQs](https://www.sos.alabama.gov/business-entities/homeowners-associations/hoa-faqs)

## Alaska

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA statute: Alaska Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act (AUCIOA), AS 34.08.010 et seq. (applies to common interest communities created after Jan 1, 1986)
- Condo statute: Covered under AUCIOA (AS 34.08); older condos may be governed by Horizontal Property Regimes Act, AS 34.07
- Coop statute: No specific coop statute found; covered by AUCIOA if structured as common interest community
- General nonprofit/org statute: Alaska Nonprofit Corporation Act, AS 10.20
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: Yes — adopted UCIOA (1982 version); one of the original UCIOA states

### Document Access & Transparency
**Under AUCIOA (AS 34.08.490):** The association must make financial and other records reasonably available for examination by any unit owner and their authorized agent. Governing documents are public records recorded with the county recorder's office.

Specific records that must be kept and made available include financial statements, minutes, and other association records. The statute is modeled on the 1982 UCIOA, which requires reasonable access without unreasonable fees.

**Inspection rights:** Unit owners or their agents may inspect association records; the association may not impose conditions that unreasonably restrict access.

### Proxy Voting
**Under AS 34.08.410:**
- Votes allocated to a unit may be cast pursuant to a proxy duly executed by a unit owner
- If a unit is owned by more than one person, each owner may vote or register protest through a duly executed proxy
- A proxy is void if it is not dated or purports to be revocable without notice
- A proxy terminates **one year** after its date unless it specifies a shorter term
- A unit owner may revoke a proxy only by actual notice of revocation to the person presiding over the meeting

**Directed vs. undirected:** The statute does not appear to restrict proxies to directed form; both appear permitted.

**No post-declarant-control proxy ban** found (unlike Arizona).

### Sources
- [Alaska UCIOA, AS 34.08](https://www.hopb.co/alaska-uniform-common-interest-ownership-act-title-34-chapter-08)
- [AS 34.08.410 – Voting and proxies](https://www.lawserver.com/law/state/alaska/ak-statutes/alaska_statutes_34-08-410)

## Arizona

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA statute (planned communities): Arizona Planned Communities Act, A.R.S. §§ 33-1801 through 33-1818
- Condo statute: Arizona Condominium Act, A.R.S. §§ 33-1201 through 33-1270
- Coop statute: No specific coop statute found; general nonprofit corporation law applies
- General nonprofit/org statute: Arizona Nonprofit Corporation Act, A.R.S. §§ 10-3101 et seq.
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: No — Arizona has its own statutes, not a UCIOA/UCA adopter

### Document Access & Transparency
**Planned communities (A.R.S. § 33-1805):** All financial and other records of the association must be made **reasonably available** for examination by any member or any person designated in writing by the member. Key rules:
- Association has **10 business days** to fulfill a records request
- Association may **not charge** a member for making records available for review (examination is free; copying costs may apply)
- Records that may be withheld: attorney-client privileged communications, pending litigation records, executive session minutes not required to be open, and other legally confidential information

**Condominiums (A.R.S. § 33-1258):** Same framework as planned communities — all financial and other records must be reasonably available for examination by any unit owner or designated agent.

**All meetings** (association, board, regularly scheduled committee) must be open to all members, with agendas provided with meeting notice.

### Proxy Voting
**Condominiums (A.R.S. § 33-1250) and Planned Communities (A.R.S. § 33-1812):**

Arizona has a **significant proxy restriction**: After the **termination of the period of declarant control**, votes allocated to a unit **may NOT be cast pursuant to a proxy**. This is a strict prohibition — proxies are only allowed during the developer control period.

**During declarant control:** Proxies are permitted and must be duly executed by the unit owner.

**Voting alternatives required:** The association must provide for votes to be cast in person and by **absentee ballot**. The association may also allow voting by email, fax, or other electronic delivery.

**Practical implication:** Arizona effectively prohibits traditional proxy voting for established HOAs and condos, substituting absentee ballots instead.

- [A.R.S. § 33-1250 – Voting; proxies (condominiums)](https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01250.htm)
- [CHDB Law – Proxy Voting in Arizona HOAs](https://www.chdblaw.com/proxy-voting-in-arizona-hoas/)

## Arkansas

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA statute: No comprehensive general HOA statute; HOAs governed by their recorded governing documents and general nonprofit/corporation law
- Condo statute: Arkansas Horizontal Property Act, Ark. Code Ann. §§ 18-13-101 et seq.
- Coop statute: No specific coop statute found
- General nonprofit/org statute: Arkansas Nonprofit Corporation Act of 1993, Ark. Code Ann. §§ 4-33-101 through 4-33-1532
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: No

### Document Access & Transparency
**HOAs:** Arkansas has no comprehensive HOA statute requiring specific document access. Governing documents (CC&Rs, bylaws, amendments) must be recorded with the county recorder's office to be enforceable, making them public land records. Beyond that, homeowner access rights depend on the association's own governing documents.

**Condominiums (Horizontal Property Act):** The Arkansas Horizontal Property Act (Ark. Code Ann. §§ 18-13-101 et seq.) governs condominiums that elect to be governed by it via a Master Deed/Declaration. Specific document access provisions are not well-detailed in secondary sources; the act is relatively lean compared to modern uniform acts.

**General nonprofit corporation:** For associations organized as nonprofit corporations, the Arkansas Nonprofit Corporation Act provides members with rights to inspect corporate records (minutes, financial records, membership lists) upon written demand.

### Proxy Voting
**General rule:** Members are permitted to vote by proxy unless the bylaws or articles of incorporation explicitly prohibit or restrict proxies. The Arkansas HOA and Horizontal Property statutes do not explicitly address proxies; the association's bylaws govern.

**Under Arkansas Nonprofit Corporation Act:** Proxy voting is generally permitted for nonprofit corporation members, subject to the association's governing documents. Arkansas has adopted electronic voting/virtual meeting statutes that allow for remote participation.

**No directed/undirected distinction** or specific proxy form requirements found in Arkansas statute — governing documents control.

## California

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA/condo statute: Davis-Stirling Common Interest Development Act, Cal. Civil Code §§ 4000–6150 (HOAs and condos)
- Coop statute: California Corporations Code §§ 12200 et seq. (Nonprofit Mutual Benefit Corporations); some coops use Cal. Corp. Code §§ 7110 et seq. (Nonprofit Public Benefit) or stock cooperative provisions
- General nonprofit/org statute: California Nonprofit Corporation Law, Cal. Corp. Code §§ 7110 et seq.
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: No — California has its own comprehensive statute (Davis-Stirling)

### Document Access & Transparency
**Under Davis-Stirling (Civil Code §§ 5200–5240):** California has among the most detailed document access regimes in the country.

**"Association records"** (§ 5200) include: financial documents required to be provided to members, interim financial statements (balance sheet, income/expense, budget comparison, general ledger), and enhanced association records.

**"Enhanced association records"** include: invoices, receipts, canceled checks, purchase orders, credit card statements, statements for services rendered, and reimbursement requests.

**Access timelines (§ 5210):**
- Current fiscal year records: must be provided within **10 business days** of request
- Records from previous two fiscal years: within **30 calendar days**

**Fees:** Association may charge for direct and actual cost of copying and mailing, but must notify the member of costs and obtain agreement **before** copying.

**Penalties:** Non-compliant HOAs may be penalized up to **$500 per request**, plus the homeowner's attorney fees and costs.

**Coop boards:** Stock cooperatives that are common interest developments are subject to Davis-Stirling. Other coops (mutual benefit corporations) are governed by Cal. Corp. Code § 8330 (right to inspect corporate records).

### Proxy Voting
**Under Davis-Stirling (Civil Code § 5130):**
- A "proxy" is a written authorization signed by a member (or authorized representative) giving another member or members the power to vote on their behalf
- **Proxyholder must be a member** of the association
- Both **directed** (specific voting instructions) and **undirected** (general authorization) proxies are permitted
- **Directed proxy procedure (§ 5130(c)):** Voting instructions must be on a **separate page** that is detached from the proxy. The proxy page goes to the Inspector of Elections; the proxyholder then casts a secret ballot per the instructions
- Association may use proxies if permitted or required by bylaws, but is not required to prepare or distribute proxies

**No post-declarant-control ban** — California allows proxies throughout the life of the association, subject to bylaws.

### Sources
- [Civil Code § 5200 – Association Records](https://www.davis-stirling.com/HOME/Statutes/Civil-Code-5200)
- [Civil Code § 5130 – Proxies](https://www.davis-stirling.com/HOME/Statutes/Civil-Code-5130)
- [LS Carlson Law – HOA Records Requests](https://lscarlsonlaw.com/articles/how-to-request-hoa-records-under-california-civil-code-section-5200/)

## Colorado

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA/condo statute: Colorado Common Interest Ownership Act (CCIOA), C.R.S. §§ 38-33.3-101 et seq.
- Coop statute: Covered under CCIOA if organized as a common interest community; otherwise Colorado Nonprofit Corporation Act
- General nonprofit/org statute: Colorado Revised Nonprofit Corporation Act, C.R.S. §§ 7-121-101 et seq.
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: Yes — CCIOA is based on UCIOA (1982 version); one of the original UCIOA adopters

### Document Access & Transparency
**Under CCIOA (C.R.S. § 38-33.3-317):** All records maintained by the association must be available for examination and copying by a unit owner or their authorized agent, subject to limited exceptions. Key rules:
- Association **may not condition** production of records upon the member stating a "proper purpose"
- Associations must retain ballots, proxies, and voting records for **one year** after the election/vote
- Records that may be withheld: attorney-client privileged communications, personnel records, individual financial records of homeowners (other than the requester's), certain pending litigation materials

Colorado's Division of Real Estate (DRE) has oversight authority over HOAs and receives complaints about non-compliance.

### Proxy Voting
**Under CCIOA (C.R.S. § 38-33.3-310):**
- Votes allocated to a unit may be cast pursuant to a **proxy duly executed** by a unit owner
- A proxy terminates **11 months** after its date unless it specifies otherwise
- A unit owner may revoke a proxy only by **actual notice of revocation** to the person presiding over the meeting
- A proxy is void if obtained through **fraud or misrepresentation**
- If a unit has multiple owners, each may vote or register protest via a duly executed proxy

**Directed vs. undirected:** Not explicitly restricted — both forms appear permitted under CCIOA.

**No post-declarant-control proxy ban** (unlike Arizona).

### Sources
- [CCIOA § 38-33.3-310 – Voting; Proxies](https://law.justia.com/codes/colorado/2022/title-38/article-33-3/part-3/section-38-33-3-310/)
- [CCIOA § 38-33.3-317 – Association Records](https://law.justia.com/codes/colorado/2018/title-38/real-property/article-33.3/part-3/section-38-33.3-317/)
- [Colorado DRE – CCIOA](https://dre.colorado.gov/ccioa-and-other-state-local-and-federal-laws)

## Connecticut

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA/condo statute: Connecticut Common Interest Ownership Act (CIOA), Conn. Gen. Stat. §§ 47-200 through 47-284 (applies to communities created on or after Jan 1, 1984)
- Older communities: Conn. Gen. Stat. § 47-261b extends CIOA rights to pre-1984 communities for events occurring after Jan 1, 1984
- Coop statute: Covered under CIOA if structured as common interest community
- General nonprofit/org statute: Connecticut Revised Nonstock Corporation Act, Conn. Gen. Stat. §§ 33-1000 et seq.
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: Yes — Connecticut adopted the 2008 UCIOA version; CIOA was the **first** unified state common interest community statute in the US (enacted 1983, effective 1984)

### Document Access & Transparency
**Under CIOA (Conn. Gen. Stat. § 47-260):** All financial and other records of the association must be **reasonably available** for examination by any unit owner and their authorized agents.

**Process:**
- Association must provide, within **5 business days** of receiving a request, two available dates for examination/copying
- Association may charge a **reasonable fee** for copies and supervision of inspection
- Copies may be provided by photocopying or **electronic transmission**

**Records that must be withheld:**
- Personnel, salary, and medical records relating to specific individuals
- Unredacted ballots, proxy forms, or other records identifying how a specific unit owner voted
- Information whose disclosure would violate any other law

**Records that may be withheld:**
- Contracts/commercial transactions being negotiated
- Existing or potential litigation
- Attorney-client communications
- Executive session records
- Individual unit files (other than the requesting owner's own file)

### Proxy Voting
**Under CIOA (Conn. Gen. Stat. § 47-252):**
- Votes may be cast pursuant to a **directed or undirected proxy** duly executed by a unit owner
- The association may provide a proxy form; if it does, the form must include a blank for the proxy holder's name and may include a **default proxy holder** designated by the association
- A proxy is void if not dated or if it purports to be revocable without notice
- A unit owner may revoke a proxy only by **actual notice of revocation** to the person presiding over the meeting
- Both directed and undirected proxies explicitly recognized — one of the clearest state statutes on this distinction

### Sources
- [CT CIOA § 47-252 – Voting, Proxies, Ballots](https://law.justia.com/codes/connecticut/title-47/chapter-828/section-47-252/)
- [CT CIOA § 47-260 – Association Records](https://law.justia.com/codes/connecticut/title-47/chapter-828/section-47-260/)
- [CAI CT – CIOA Overview](https://www.caict.org/page/CIOA)

## Delaware

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA/condo/coop statute: Delaware Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act (DUCIOA), 25 Del. C. §§ 81-101 et seq. (covers HOAs, condos, and coops as "common interest communities")
- General nonprofit/org statute: Delaware General Corporation Law / Nonprofit Corporation Act
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: Yes — adopted 2008 UCIOA; one of the more recent UCIOA adopters

### Document Access & Transparency
**Under DUCIOA (25 Del. C. § 81-317):** All records maintained by the association must be available for examination and copying by a unit owner or their authorized agent. The association has the right to charge a fee, in advance, for conversion, inspection, or copying of association records.

**Governing documents** (declarations, bylaws, rules) are recorded with the county recorder and are public land records. Electronic access is also available.

**Records that may be withheld:** Attorney-client privileged communications, personnel records, pending litigation materials, and other legally confidential records (consistent with UCIOA framework).

### Proxy Voting
**Under DUCIOA (25 Del. C. § 81-310):**
- Votes allocated to a unit may be cast pursuant to a proxy duly executed by a unit owner
- A proxy is void if not dated or if it purports to be revocable without notice
- A proxy terminates **1 year** after its date unless it specifies a shorter term
- Votes may be expressed: (i) in person, (ii) by proxy (if governing documents allow), (iii) electronically from a registered email/source, (iv) by electronic voting software, (v) by signing a petition calling for an amendment, or (vi) by signing an amendment to the governing document

**No post-declarant-control proxy ban** found.

## Florida

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA statute: Florida Homeowners' Association Act, Fla. Stat. Ch. 720
- Condo statute: Florida Condominium Act, Fla. Stat. Ch. 718
- Coop statute: Florida Cooperative Act, Fla. Stat. Ch. 719
- General nonprofit/org statute: Florida Not for Profit Corporation Act, Fla. Stat. Ch. 617
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: No — Florida has its own comprehensive statutes

### Document Access & Transparency
**HOAs (Ch. 720):** Official records must be made available to parcel owners for inspection or copying within **10 business days** of a written request. Records must be physically available within 45 miles of the community or within the county where the association is located; electronic access is an acceptable alternative. Associations may adopt reasonable rules about frequency, time, and manner of inspection. **Owners do not need to state a reason** for requesting records. Associations must allow use of portable scanning devices. Reasonable copying fees may be charged.

**Condominiums (Fla. Stat. § 718.111):** Association must maintain official records from the inception of the association, including plans, permits, warranties, developer documents, minutes, financial records, and voting records. Records available for inspection with right to copy at reasonable expense. **Failure to provide records within 10 working days** of a written request creates a rebuttable presumption of willful non-compliance. Associations may not require members to state a purpose for the inspection. Voting proxies and ballots must be maintained for **1 year** from the date of the election.

### Proxy Voting
**HOAs (Fla. Stat. § 720.306):**
- Members may vote in person or by proxy (unless governing documents provide otherwise)
- Proxy must be dated, state the date/time/place of the meeting, and be signed by the executing member
- Proxy is effective only for the **specific meeting** for which given (including adjournments), and automatically expires **90 days** after the date of that meeting
- Proxy is revocable at any time by the person who executed it
- If proxy form expressly allows it, the proxy holder may appoint a written substitute
- **General proxies** are permitted for HOA votes; no directed/undirected restriction
- **No prohibition on proxy use for board elections** in HOAs (unlike condominiums)

**Condominiums (Fla. Stat. Ch. 718):**
- Unit owners in **residential condominiums may NOT vote by general proxy**; they may only vote by **limited proxy** (essentially an absentee ballot), substantially conforming to a form adopted by the Division of Florida Condominiums
- **Proxies are prohibited** for board director elections in condominiums
- Limited proxies are allowed for substantive owner votes (e.g., amendments, special assessments)
- This is a **significant distinction** from HOAs — Florida condos have much stricter proxy restrictions

All community documents must be made **reasonably available** to association members; copies must be made and sent upon written request. The HOA may charge for **reasonable expenses** of making copies.

- Appointment must be **in writing and executed**
- Appointment is effective when the association's secretary or other officer receives it
- By default, a proxy lasts **11 months** unless stated otherwise
- A proxy's validity **cannot be extended beyond three years**

## Nevada

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA/condo/coop statute: Nevada Common-Interest Ownership (Uniform Act), NRS Ch. 116 (comprehensive statute covering HOAs, condos, and coops as "common-interest communities")
- General nonprofit/org statute: Nevada Nonprofit Corporation Law, NRS Ch. 82
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: Yes — NRS Ch. 116 is Nevada's version of the UCIOA (though significantly modified); Nevada was an early adopter

### Document Access & Transparency
**Under NRS 116.31175 (General records requirements):**
- Executive board must provide copies of records to a unit owner or the Ombudsman within **21 days** after receiving a written request
- Associations may **not charge more than $25 per hour** to review books, records, contracts, or other papers
- Records must be maintained at the association's business office or another suitable location within the county

**Under NRS 116.3118 (Financial records and inspection rights):**
- All financial and other records must be **reasonably available** for any unit owner and their authorized agents to inspect, examine, photocopy, and audit
- Association must keep financial records sufficiently detailed to comply with resale disclosure requirements (NRS 116.4109)

**Nevada Ombudsman:** Nevada has a Real Estate Division Ombudsman for Common-Interest Communities with enforcement authority over records access violations — one of the strongest administrative enforcement mechanisms in the country.

### Proxy Voting
**Under NRS 116.311:**
- Votes allocated to a unit may be cast pursuant to a proxy executed by the unit owner
- A unit owner may give a proxy **only to**: (a) a member of their immediate family, (b) a tenant who resides in the community, (c) another unit owner who resides in the community, or (d) a delegate/representative when authorized — this is a **significant restriction** on who may hold a proxy
- **Proxies are prohibited for board elections**: a vote may NOT be cast by proxy for the election or removal of a member of the executive board (limited exceptions for master associations)
- The proxy holder may **not cast a vote contrary** to the proxy — effectively requiring directed proxies
- A proxy is void if the proxy or holder violates any provision of the statute
- A unit owner may revoke a proxy only by actual notice of revocation to the person presiding over the meeting
- No votes allocated to a unit owned by the association may be cast, by proxy or otherwise

**Nevada has some of the strictest proxy rules in the country** — limited eligible proxy holders, no proxies for board elections, and mandatory directed form.

## Virginia

### Applicable Statutes
- HOA statute: Virginia Property Owners' Association Act, Va. Code §§ 55.1-1800 et seq. (Title 55.1, Ch. 18)
- Condo statute: Virginia Condominium Act, Va. Code §§ 55.1-1900 et seq. (Title 55.1, Ch. 19)
- Coop statute: Virginia Real Estate Cooperative Act, Va. Code §§ 55.1-2100 et seq. (Title 55.1, Ch. 21)
- General nonprofit/org statute: Virginia Nonstock Corporation Act, Va. Code §§ 13.1-801 et seq.
- UCIOA/UCA adoption: No — Virginia has its own comprehensive statutes (among the most detailed in the country)

### Document Access & Transparency
**HOAs (Va. Code § 55.1-1815):** Lot owners have the right of access to **all books and records** kept by or on behalf of the association, subject to limited exceptions. Key rules:
- Records must be kept in accordance with **generally accepted accounting practices**
- Draft minutes of board meetings must be open for inspection and copying within **60 days** from the conclusion of the meeting (or when distributed to board members for the next meeting agenda)
- Records that may be withheld: personnel matters, medical records, contracts/leases under negotiation, pending/probable litigation, individual lot owner files (other than the requester's)

**Condominiums (Va. Code § 55.1-1945 et seq.):** Similar detailed records access requirements. Financial books and records must be kept and made available to unit owners.

**Coops (Va. Code § 55.1-2100 et seq.):** The Real Estate Cooperative Act provides similar protections for cooperative members.

### Proxy Voting
**HOAs (Va. Code § 55.1-1815):** A member may vote at a meeting of the association in person, by **proxy**, or by **absentee ballot**. Electronic voting is permitted if the board has adopted guidelines for it.

**Condominiums (Va. Code § 55.1-1953):**
- Votes may be cast pursuant to a **proxy duly executed** by or on behalf of the unit owner (or, for multiple owners, by all such persons)
- Voting by proxy, absentee ballot, or **electronic means** is permitted (if executive board has adopted guidelines)
- Unit owners voting by absentee ballot or proxy are **deemed present** at the meeting for all purposes (including quorum)

**No post-declarant-control proxy ban** found. Directed vs. undirected distinction not explicitly addressed — governing documents control.

## Washington

- HOA/condo/coop statute: Washington Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act (WUCIOA), RCW Ch. 64.90 (comprehensive statute covering HOAs, condos, coops; applies to communities formed after July 1, 2018; older communities may opt in or are grandfathered)

- UCIOA/UCA adoption: Yes — WUCIOA is Washington's 2008 UCIOA adoption; one of the most recent state adopters

### Document Access & Transparency
**Under WUCIOA (RCW 64.90):** Records of the association must be provided to owners upon request within **10 days**, unless more time is reasonably required, but **never exceeding 21 days** absent a court order. This is one of the more specific timelines in the country.

### Proxy Voting
**Under D.C. Code § 42-1903.05 (Allocation of Votes; Proxies):**

**Directed vs. undirected:** Not explicitly restricted — both appear permitted.
